# Pricing

Claiming a .zcash name involves sending ZEC to a registry wallet with the name price depending on how long the name is. (Shorter names cost more because there are fewer of them.)

## What a name costs

| Name length | Cost (ZEC) |
|-------------|-----------|
| 1 character | 6.00 |
| 2 characters | 4.25 |
| 3 characters | 3.00 |
| 4 characters | 1.50 |
| 5 characters | 0.75 |
| 6 characters | 0.50 |
| 7 characters or more | 0.25 |

These are the current default tiers on testnet 

## How you pay

When you claim a name, the [ZcashNames site](https://www.zcashnames.com) generates a payment URI with the exact amount and the signed claim memo. 

You send the transaction from any wallet that supports ZIP-321 and Orchard memos, see [Wallet Compatibility](/docs/use/wallets)) and that's the payment for name-registration.

## Why tiered pricing

Shorter names are more valuable to attackers and squatters. Tiered pricing makes it expensive enough to deter casual squatting on `a` or `bob` while keeping ordinary 7+ character names cheap enough to be worth it for individual users.

Pricing is adjusted by broadcasting an admin-signed `SETPRICE` memo to the chain. Every honest indexer will pick it up at the block it lands in and update the ZNS pricing tier.

## Marketplace pricing

If you buy a name that's already registered and listed for sale, you pay the **listing price**, not the claim cost. 

Listing prices are set by the seller and can be anything they like (with a small minimum). See [Buying & Selling](/docs/use/buying-and-selling) for the marketplace flow.

## Renewal

Unlike traditional name-registration, ZNS has no renewal fees. Once you've paid the claim cost, you own the name unless you decide to release it. 

## Reading current prices

Wallets and apps may display the live pricing tier by reading it from the indexer's `status` RPC method, in the `pricing.tiers` field. See [JSON-RPC Reference](/docs/indexer/rpc-reference#status) for the exact response shape, or use one of the [SDK ports](/docs/sdk).

For the protocol-level details (`SETPRICE` memo grammar, the bootstrap requirement, trust-minimized pricing verification, USD display), see [Pricing (Protocol)](/docs/protocol/pricing).
